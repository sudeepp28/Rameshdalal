The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 — popularly known as the POSH Act — was enacted over a decade ago. Yet, even today, non-compliance is widespread, especially in startups, MSMEs, and decentralized organizations.

📌 Let’s be very clear:

Non-compliance with POSH provisions is not just unethical — it’s illegal.

🔍 What the POSH Act Mandates:

  • Formation of an Internal Committee (IC) at every office/branch with 10 or more employees
  • Inclusion of an external member (NGO or legal expert) in the IC
  • Display of POSH policy at a conspicuous place in the office
  • Conducting awareness sessions for all employees
  • Training of IC members to handle complaints effectively
  • Annual reporting of complaints and redressals to the District Officer

🚫 Key Areas Where Many Employers Still Fail:

  • No properly constituted Internal Committee
  • Ignoring the need for an external member
  • No awareness or sensitization sessions held regularly
  • Belief that “no complaints” means no action required
  • Failure to file the Annual Report

🧩 It’s Not Just a Woman’s Issue — It’s a Workplace Issue.

Workplace safety and dignity is the foundation of a healthy organizational culture. It impacts:

  • Trust in leadership
  • Employer branding
  • Employee retention
  • Risk of reputational & legal damage

🛠️ What Should Organizations Do Proactively?

  • Revisit your POSH policy — Is it legally sound and practically implementable?
  • Train your IC members — Not just once, but periodically.
  • Create a safe environment — Not just physically, but psychologically.
  • Take every complaint seriously — Even informal ones.
  • Document everything properly — For legal and audit readiness.

⚖️ Non-Compliance Can Lead To:

  • Monetary Fines
  • Cancellation of business licenses
  • Civil/criminal liabilities depending on the case
  • Irreparable damage to your brand

🔔 Compliance isn’t a checkbox. It’s a commitment.
